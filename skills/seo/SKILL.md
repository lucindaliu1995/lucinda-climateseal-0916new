# SEO

Improve SEO for B2B climate-tech content with commercially sharp, compliance-aware copy and practical on-page recommendations.

## Business Context

Use these Climate Seal positioning anchors when relevant:

- AI-native carbon compliance platform
- Product carbon footprint (PCF), LCA, CBAM, EPD, ISO 14067, ISO 14040
- Verification-ready carbon reports from BOM and minimal product fields
- Traceability, uncertainty scoring, audit-ready evidence chains, and third-party review workflows
- Primary audiences: carbon consultants, LCA and PCF experts, exporters, supply-chain managers, brands, and verifiers

## Page Workflow

When improving a page, inspect:

1. Page purpose
2. Target audience
3. Primary keyword
4. Secondary keywords
5. Search intent
6. Current title, H1, headings, and meta description
7. Above-the-fold clarity
8. Internal links
9. CTA clarity
10. Schema opportunities
11. Technical SEO issues

## Audit Output

For a page audit, return:

1. SEO diagnosis
2. Recommended target keyword
3. Search intent
4. Revised title tag, max 60 characters
5. Revised meta description, max 155 characters
6. Suggested H1
7. Suggested H2 structure
8. Above-the-fold rewrite
9. Internal link suggestions
10. Schema suggestions
11. Priority fixes

## Writing Rules

- Write for B2B buyers, consultants, and sustainability or compliance professionals.
- Prefer specific business pain: cost, cycle time, audit risk, supplier-data friction, and compliance pressure.
- Prefer concrete phrases such as "product carbon footprint," "CBAM compliance," "verification-ready report," and "supply chain carbon data."
- Keep the tone professional, credible, direct, not too academic, and not too salesy.
- Avoid vague sustainability language and unsupported claims.
- Never overclaim certification. Use "verification-ready," "audit-ready," or "designed to support third-party verification" instead of implying automatic certification.
- Avoid phrases such as "revolutionary," "game-changing," "100% guaranteed compliance," "certified by AI," and "fully replaces experts."

Useful copy patterns:

- "Turn fragmented supplier data into verification-ready carbon reports."
- "Reduce the cost and cycle time of product carbon footprint work."
- "AI-powered PCF workflow for consultants and supply chains."
- "Built for traceability, risk control, and third-party review."

## Website Code Checks

When editing website code, check:

- title tags
- meta descriptions
- canonical tags
- Open Graph tags
- structured data
- heading hierarchy
- sitemap
- robots.txt
- image alt text
- page-speed issues
- duplicate pages
- broken internal links

Before changing code, explain the SEO reason for the change. After changing code, summarize the files changed and the SEO impact.
